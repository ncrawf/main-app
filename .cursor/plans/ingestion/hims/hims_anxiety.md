Propranolol for Anxiety
Perhaps you have some important meetings coming up, a speaking engagement, or an interview. Propranolol is a drug sometimes prescribed by doctors to help control the physical symptoms of performance anxiety.
Start my free visit
Important Safety Information
Propranolol is not FDA-approved for the treatment of performance anxiety, situational anxiety or other anxiety. Learn more about off-label uses
Prescription products are subject to healthcare provider approval and require an online consultation with a healthcare provider after checkout who will determine if a prescription is appropriate. Take the medication as prescribed.
Common side effects of propranolol include nausea, diarrhea, constipation, stomach cramps, decreased sex drive, impotence, difficulty having an orgasm, insomnia, or feeling tired. Do not take propranolol if you are allergic to it, have asthma, a very slow heart beat that has caused you to faint, a serious heart condition such as "sick sinus syndrome" or "AV block" (unless you have a pacemaker), liver problems or low blood sugar.
Call your doctor immediately if you have slow or uneven heartbeats, a light-headed feeling like you might pass out, wheezing or trouble breathing, shortness of breath, swelling, or rapid weight gain, sudden vision problems, or loss of coordination, cold feeling in your hands and feet, depression, hallucinations or confusion, liver problems (nausea, itching, fatigue, loss of appetite, dark urine, clay-colored stools), jaundice, low blood sugar headache, hunger, weakness, sweating, confusion, irritability, dizziness, fast heart rate, feeling jittery, fever, facial swelling, skin pain and rash associated with blistering.

Medical Note:
Propranolol is a beta blocker approved by the FDA to treat high blood pressure, angina, and certain other conditions, but it has not been specifically approved to treat anxiety. Use of Propranolol to treat anxiety is known as an "off-label use". A provider is permitted to prescribe a medication for an off-label use when they determine it is medically appropriate for the patient. Propranolol off-label use is intended to treat the physical symptoms of performance or situational anxiety and will not be effective in treating generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) or social anxiety disorder (SAD). By continuing, you acknowledge and agree that your provider may prescribe Propranolol to you for this off-label use.
